## Vision Statement
FinTech is a collaborative web application designed to help fish enthusiasts understand water conditions and how they influence aquatic life, and where they can find a body of water with similar conditions. 

---
## Motivation
It can be challenging to understand how water conditions affect fish populations. Finding bodies of water with specific or similar environmental conditions can be time-consuming.

Educational content related to fishes are usually very straightforward and uninteractive. They are usually scattered and disjointed. Collecting data on various types of fish and their shared condition allows a new avenue of education by giving users the ability to see what types of fish can share the habitat (theoretically). 

---
## Risks to Project Completion
- **Scheduling and Coordination Challenges:** Members of this group are all working, so availability is heavily restricted 
- **Feature Scope Creep:** There is a risk of trying to implement too many features to visualise the data associated. 
- **Learning Curve with Full-Stack Integration:** Some team members have limited experience connecting frontend interfaces with backend APIs and databases.
- Limited field knowledge: We’re working on the subject of fish and water, which our group is not familiar with.
  
---
## Mitigation Strategy
- **Asynchronous Communication:** The team will rely on weekly scheduled asynchronous meetings through Zoom and Trello for project tracking.
- **Minimal Viable Product First:** Initial development will be simple at first by allowing users to see water conditions, the fish information, and where to find similar bodies of water before adding more complex features. 
- **Research Driven Development:** The team will rely on environmental, fish resources to present accurate information on fish and water conditions. 

---
## Development Method
The team will follow an Agile approach with weekly meetings to address tasks and blockers. Scrum methodology will be needed here to first break down our projects into parts, and to divide each component of the projects into a splittable chunk for each member to do. A kanban board will also help further divide the work by showing everyone what still needs to be done and what has already been done. 

---
## Development Steps
1. Define core user stories and functional requirements. 
2. Design database schema for each type of fish and conditions for body of waters
3. Implement backend API endpoints for filtering out fish based on conditions.
4. Build frontend views for the water system and fish art.  
5. Implement third party API or database to list out locations/body of waters 
6. Conduct testing of our app and prepare final presentation and documentation.

---
## Project Tracking
The team will use Trello to track tasks, sprint progress, and blockers. Tasks will be labeled by priority and assigned to individual team members to maintain accountability.
